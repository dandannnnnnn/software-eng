## How to Study Software Engineering (First-Year Level)

### 1 Understand the Nature of the Course

Software Engineering is **concept-heavy + application-based**.
You are usually tested on:

* SDLC models (Waterfall, Agile, Scrum…)
* Requirements engineering
* UML diagrams
* Testing (unit, integration, system)
* Design principles (SOLID basics, cohesion/coupling)
* Project management basics

This is not memorization only. You must **understand relationships and trade-offs**.

---

## Best Study Strategy

### Step 1: Study Actively (Not Passive Reading)

- Don’t reread slides.
- Do this instead:

* After each topic, close your notes and ask:

  * *“Can I explain this to someone?”*
* Summarize each chapter on **one page maximum**.
* Create comparison tables (e.g., Waterfall vs Agile).

---

### Step 2: Practice Application

Most students lose points here.

For each topic, ask:

* When would I use this?
* What are advantages/disadvantages?
* What problem does this solve?

Example:
Instead of memorizing Scrum roles, ask:

> Why does Scrum separate Product Owner and Scrum Master?

---

### Step 3: Use Retrieval Practice

Best method for university:

* Make flashcards (Anki if possible).
* Practice old exam questions.
* Write definitions from memory.

---

### Step 4: Practice UML Actively

Don’t just recognize diagrams.
You should be able to:

* Draw use case diagrams
* Draw class diagrams
* Explain relationships (association, inheritance, aggregation)

---

## 7-Day Exam Plan (Example)

**Day 1–2:** SDLC models + Requirements
**Day 3:** UML
**Day 4:** Testing + Quality
**Day 5:** Design principles
**Day 6:** Practice exam questions
**Day 7:** Weak topics review + active recall only

---

## Important Mindset

Software Engineering questions often ask:

* Compare
* Explain why
* Analyze a situation
* Identify best approach

So always study in this format:

> Concept → Why → When → Advantages → Disadvantages
